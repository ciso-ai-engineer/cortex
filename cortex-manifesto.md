# **CORTEX Manifesto V0.1**

## **CORTEX – A Living System for Human-AI Collaboration**

By [Jake Martin](https://www.linkedin.com/in/seattlesearchoptimization/)

*From "March or Die" to "Constant Evolution" – A Manifesto for 20 Years of Agility, Adaptability, and Intelligence*

---

## **1\. Philosophy**

CORTEX is not a framework. It is a **living system** \- a philosophy, methodology, and set of tools to co-create with AI, sustain knowledge, and evolve work dynamically.

Where Agile and Scrum optimized human collaboration through iteration, CORTEX optimizes **human-machine synthesis** through **intelligent artifacts**, **self-forming teams**, and **continuous evolution**.

CORTEX is:

* AI-native

* Outcome-first

* Artifact-driven

* Role-fluid

* Cognitively hygienic

---

## **2\. Core Tenets**

### **1\. Everything is an Artifact**

Prompts, Objectives, Tests, Knowledge \- all are first-class, versioned, and tracked. They are executable units of value.

### **2\. Knowledge is Alive**

Every piece of documentation, code, prompt, and video decays or evolves. Nothing is static. All content has versioning, telemetry, and review cycles.

### **3\. AI is a Co-Developer**

AI agents scaffold, test, and document. But humans retain authority, interpret ambiguity, and approve evolution.

### **4\. Pods Emerge**

Teams assemble dynamically around objectives. Roles like Synthesist, Engineer, and OpsDev fluidly shape, contribute, and review. No rigid team assignments.

### **5\. Feedback is Fuel**

Every user touchpoint \- telemetry, support tickets, or NPS \- feeds back into system evolution through FeedbackOps.

### **6\. Cognitive Hygiene is Enforced**

YAML-based artifacts include metadata for decay scores, last-reviewed dates, and deprecation plans. AI and humans co-manage knowledge lifecycle.

### **7\. Interface Doesn’t Matter**

With a Universal Object Model (UOM), prompt, test, feedback, and KB structures can be rendered in any language or interface. The model is the contract.

## **3\. Methodology Overview**

### **A. Objective Cards**

Objective Cards are the fundamental unit of intent and alignment in CORTEX. They define a business or system outcome in clear, value-driven terms. Objective Cards trigger the formation of dynamic pods and guide all downstream action. They are measurable, reviewable, and self-contained. Each Objective is born from signals-user needs, internal strategy, or telemetry-and serves as the contract for what should be accomplished.

### 

### **B. PromptOps**

PromptOps is the discipline of designing, evaluating, and governing interactions between humans and AI agents. It standardizes how we frame intent, constrain model behavior, and validate the quality of AI outputs. PromptOps ensures repeatability and interpretability, aligning LLM performance with organizational standards and compliance. It includes practices such as prompt evaluation, hallucination control, and traceability across versions.

### **C. Test Cards**

Test Cards represent executable, declarative specifications of how an outcome should behave under real conditions. They are not written only by engineers-they can be authored by anyone in the pod. Test Cards are the enforcers of trust, defining acceptance criteria that AI-generated code or human work must meet. They enable simulation, regression checks, and integration into automated decision systems.

### **D. FeedbackOps**

FeedbackOps is the routing and transformation of user insight, system telemetry, and stakeholder input into structured signals for system improvement. It closes the loop between delivery and outcome by mapping every complaint, confusion, or delight back to the Objectives and Prompts that need attention. FeedbackOps ensures that CORTEX remains evolutionary and grounded in real-world use.

### **E. Knowledge Base \+ Documentation**

As a byproduct of completing Objectives and running tests, CORTEX produces persistent knowledge in the form of structured documentation. This includes structured knowledge base entries derived from artifacts and test coverage. While video generation is a possibility and may support training or visual verification, it is not required or assumed as a core part of this methodology. The emphasis is on sustainable, traceable, and human-verified documentation that evolves with the system.

## **5\. Governance \+ Lifecycle Standards**

CORTEX treats governance not as a control layer but as a framework for sustainability, trust, and evolution. Every artifact \- whether a prompt, test, Objective, or knowledge document \- exists in a lifecycle of relevance. This lifecycle must be governed not only by logic or automation, but also by human insight and strategic intent.

Governance in CORTEX is about establishing durable systems of accountability without stifling agility. It respects the necessity of oversight while embracing decentralization and self-forming teams. It ensures every contributor \- human or AI \- can trace, justify, and improve the work.

Lifecycle standards reinforce CORTEX’s philosophy that **knowledge must live or be retired**. This includes:

* Defining states of vitality for each artifact (e.g., active, stale, deprecated).

* Establishing principles for artifact decay \- how age, usage, and feedback inform whether something is still useful.

* Creating mechanisms for graceful deprecation, including handoffs, archival rules, and references to superseding work.

* Supporting human-in-the-loop checkpoints for high-impact transitions (e.g., promotion to canonical, retirement from active rotation).

* Fostering transparency through traceability \- knowing when something changed, why, and who decided.

Governance is not an overlay. It is a living agreement between evolving knowledge, machine collaborators, and human authors. Lifecycle management is its operational form \- a durable scaffolding for everything else in CORTEX.

## **6\. 20-Year Vision: Living System, Human-Aligned**

* CORTEX must scale across orgs, AI agents, and modalities

* It must support DAOs, fluid pods, sovereign dev orgs

* It must measure truth, decay, and divergence \- not just velocity

* It must teach itself how to evolve through usage and failure

*“CORTEX is not here to manage software. It’s here to cultivate intelligence.”*
